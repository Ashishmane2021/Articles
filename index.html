An Initial Public Offering (IPO) marks a significant milestone in a company’s journey — the moment it opens its doors to public investors. Through an IPO, a private company offers its shares to the general public for the first time, allowing investors to become part-owners of the business. For investors, IPOs present exciting opportunities to invest in companies at an early stage of their public life. However, as with all investments, IPOs come with potential rewards and risks that must be carefully evaluated.
